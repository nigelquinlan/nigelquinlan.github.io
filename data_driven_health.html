Data Driven Health

Nurse unit managers from across Epworth were spending several hours a month chasing up-to-date information about mandatory training completed or appearing as due for staff that report to them.

Better data for managers

The challenge with this project was to create better ways to serve the right information in the right way at the right time for these busy managers.

This project started out as a 'new ways of working' program sponsored by the People & Culture team at Epworth. The objective of introducing new ways of working was to apply new approaches to solving the most complex workplace challenges and to make a genuine difference for patients and staff. To achieve this, I worked with a coalition of the willing to help them to foster their curiosity and improve their openness to change.

We started with a guided journey through the Design Thinking process. Design Thinking is a problem exploring and ideas development methodology that is centred around people. It starts with a discover phase where a team will explore the challenge and conduct some research. Then they define the problem they want to solve in a way that leaves multiple potential solutions. Next they develop potential solutions and finally when a solution is tested and, potentially, has gone through several iterations, comes a deliver phase. In the deliver phase, the team might hand over to a project team to implement ths solution into the organisation or they might work alongside a business team to help with the implementation.

During this problem exploration phase, one team had a vision for a better way of handling mandatory training data at a ward level. In a hospital environment, mandatory training ranges from hand washing to potentially life saving interventions. All mandatory training is closely monitored by managers and is reported to the executive team on a monthly basis. This team had identified a potential for improving the availability and format of training data. serving up mandatory training data at a ward level. During a design phase I worked closely with this team to design and develop a dashboard for nurse unit managers. We developed a minimum viable product (MVP) and this went into use on a selected ward as a proof of concept.

The legacy at Epworth was empowerment of key individuals to drive a new culture of collaboration and innovation. The outcome of our proof of concept was shared with managers and the team responsible for building out information dashboards.
